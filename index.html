About Me

I am a Modelling Research Fellow in the Department of Infectious Diseases at the Peter Doherty Institute for Infection and Immunity, University of Melbourne. My current research focus includes infectious disease modelling to inform policy, multi-strain pathogens, and agent-based models. I am actively working on evaluating the efficacy of pneumococcal vaccination schedules using computational modelling. During my PhD at the School of Computing and Information Systems at the University of Melbourne, I worked on the transmission of Sarcoptes scabiei and group A Streptococcus and estimated efficacy of interventions using individual-based models. I has also been involved in the Doherty Institute COVID-19 modelling team, worked on the risk assessment of COVID-19 spread in Australia and intervention strategies to reduce COVID-19 burden for Australian First Nation communities.
